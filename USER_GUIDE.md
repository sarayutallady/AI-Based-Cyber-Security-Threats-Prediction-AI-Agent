## ðŸ” Understanding Threat Severity

### Critical (Red)
- **Impact**: Severe, immediate action required
- **Examples**: APT attacks, zero-day exploits, major data breaches
- **Response**: Immediate investigation and mitigation

### High (Orange)
- **Impact**: Significant, requires prompt attention
- **Examples**: Ransomware, botnets, cryptojacking
- **Response**: Prioritize investigation within hours

### Medium (Blue)
- **Impact**: Moderate, routine monitoring
- **Examples**: Phishing attempts, SQL injection, XSS attacks
- **Response**: Monitor and address during regular operations

### Low (Green)
- **Impact**: Minor or resolved
- **Examples**: Blocked attempts, resolved incidents
- **Response**: Log for records, no immediate action needed

## ðŸ“ˆ Key Metrics Explained

### Threat Count
- Total number of detected threats
- Updated in real-time
- Includes all severity levels

### Detection Rate
- Percentage of threats successfully detected
- Based on ML model performance
- Higher is better (target: >90%)

### Response Time
- Average time to detect and respond to threats
- Measured in seconds or minutes
- Lower is better

### False Positive Rate
- Percentage of false alarms
- Based on ML model accuracy
- Lower is better (target: <5%)
